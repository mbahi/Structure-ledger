How this is computed

Multi-timeframe strip — 1m/5m/15m/1H are streamed and analyzed independently, all the time, side by side. Each timeframe has its own genuinely different structure — switching the main chart doesn't "change" a setup, it's reading a different one. Use the strip to check for confluence across timeframes before you rely on any single one.
Swings — fractal highs/lows (2 bars either side). BOS — close breaks the last swing with the prevailing trend. CHoCH — close breaks against it, flipping the tracked bias.
FVG Confluence strategy — Entry at the near edge of an unmitigated 3-bar imbalance formed with the latest break, SL beyond the invalidating swing, TP1/TP2 at the next opposing swings.
SBR/RBS Retest strategy — after a broken swing level, waits for price to actually return and retest that level (Resistance-Become-Support for bullish breaks, Support-Become-Resistance for bearish breaks) before building the ticket — a slower, retest-confirmed entry rather than an immediate one.
SBR/RBS (EA Logic) — ported directly from the validated Volatility_SBR_RBS_EA.mq5: a swing (5-bar lookback) sweeps beyond, then the next confirmed close reclaims back inside it — a failed breakout, not a continuation. Entry is the sweep candle's body (order block), SL is the original swing level ± a small buffer, TP1/TP2 are fixed 1R/2.5R. Four confluence rules, independently toggleable exactly as in the EA: FVG straddling the sweep candle, a mitigation block (opposite-colour candle just before it), H4 trend agreement, D1 non-conflict. Structure is fixed to H1/H4/D1, regardless of the timeframe strip above. Backtest for this one pulls H1, H4, and D1 history together and walks forward using only H4/D1 candles that had already fully closed by each H1 bar's time — no future HTF data leaking into the past. Silver Bullet (ICT) — the actual defined concept: three specific 1-hour NY-time windows (03:00, 10:00, 14:00), DST-handled via the browser's own timezone conversion rather than a fixed UTC offset — this applies to historical bars too, so the backtest checks each 5m bar's real NY time correctly across DST changes, not an approximation. Only inside a window does it look for a sweep of a prior 5-minute swing high/low, occurring within that same window, followed by a confirmed close back inside and an FVG on the reversal. Runs on 5m data specifically, independent of the timeframe strip. Backtest fetches 5m history in pages sized for that granularity (far less time per page than H1) and skips every bar outside the three windows before checking the pattern at all — same outlier-cap and episode-independence scrutiny should be applied to its results as anything else here, since it borrows swing-based TP targeting from FVG Confluence rather than EA Logic's fixed-R:R protection against extreme outcomes.
